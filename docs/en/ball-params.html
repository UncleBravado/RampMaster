Ball Parameters — Reference

ParameterDescription
MassBall mass in grams. BISFed range: 263–287 g. A heavier ball has more momentum — harder to stop and displaces the jack more on impact.
DiameterBall diameter in millimetres. BISFed range: 79–93 mm. A larger ball has a higher centre of gravity and greater contact area.
Hardness GradeScale 1–10. A soft ball (1) has a granule fill that deforms on impact — less bounce, more absorption. A hard ball (10) behaves more elastically. Automatically sets the Crr value.
Floor FrictionFriction between the ball and the floor. Higher values slow the ball faster and reduce sliding on contact.
Base RestitutionBounce coefficient at low impact speed (0–30%). Real boccia balls have very low restitution — they barely bounce off the floor or each other.
Surface GripHow strongly the ball grips the floor while rolling. Higher grip = stronger spin and curve effect.
Rolling Resistance (Crr)Constant braking force proportional to mass and gravity (F = Crr × m × g). The main factor determining rolling distance. Auto-derived from Hardness Grade but can be overridden manually.
Velocity Drag (Cvd)Braking force proportional to speed (F = Cvd × v). Stronger at high speed, weaker when slow — simulates aerodynamic drag and surface deformation.
Angular DampingHow quickly the ball's spin decays. Higher values = ball stops rotating sooner after impact.
Ball-to-Ball FrictionFriction at ball-to-ball contact. Affects energy transfer and deflection angle on collision.
Spin SensitivityHow strongly the ball reacts to its own rotation — curves the trajectory when spin is high.
Spin Curve FactorLateral force generated by ball rotation (Magnus effect). Higher values = more pronounced curve.
Spin Transfer on ImpactWhat fraction of angular momentum is transferred to the other ball or the jack on collision.
Ball ColorVisual colour of the ball on the court.
